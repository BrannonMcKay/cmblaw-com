What is a Knockout Search?

A knockout search is a quick preliminary search of the federal trademark database. Its purpose is to identify obvious conflicts early — marks that are identical or very similar to your proposed brand name — before investing in a full clearance search.

Think of it as a first filter. If an identical live mark exists in your same industry, that's a strong signal you may need to reconsider. A knockout search can save you time and money by eliminating clearly unavailable names early.

Knockout vs. Comprehensive Clearance:

  • Knockout search — Checks the federal register for identical and phonetically similar marks. Fast, free, and useful as a first step.
  • Comprehensive clearance — Searches federal and state registers, common law sources, domain names, business registries, and social media. Includes a legal opinion on likelihood of confusion. Performed by an attorney.

Why Dead Marks Still Matter

A "dead" trademark status means the registration has been cancelled, expired, or the application was abandoned. However, dead marks are not automatically safe to use.

  • The owner may still have common law rights from ongoing use of the mark in commerce, even without a federal registration.
  • The mark may have been abandoned at the USPTO but still be actively used in a specific geographic area.
  • A dead registration can indicate that someone has previously claimed rights in a similar mark, which could complicate your application.
  • The former owner could refile a new application based on continued use.

Always evaluate dead marks carefully, especially those in related goods/services classes.

Limitations & Disclaimer

This tool provides access to the USPTO Trademark Center search. Please note the following limitations:

  • This search covers the federal trademark register only. It does not include state registrations, common law marks, or pending intent-to-use applications that haven't been published.
  • Trademark conflicts are assessed based on likelihood of confusion, not just identical matches. Similar-sounding names, translations, and stylistic variations can all create conflicts.
  • Search results may not capture every relevant mark due to variations in spelling, design elements, or classification.
  • This tool is for informational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. No attorney-client relationship is formed by using this tool.
⚠ Important: A knockout search is not a substitute for a comprehensive trademark clearance search conducted by a qualified intellectual property attorney. Filing a trademark application without proper clearance can result in refusal, opposition proceedings, or infringement claims.

Quick Search Tips

  • Search variations — Try phonetic equivalents, abbreviations, and alternate spellings (e.g., "Kool" vs "Cool").
  • Check related classes — Your mark might conflict with marks in adjacent industries, not just your exact class.
  • Search word marks separately — If your brand has multiple words, search each independently.
  • Note serial numbers — Write down the serial numbers of any concerning marks for your attorney to review.
  • Look at the owner — Large companies with similar marks are more likely to enforce their rights aggressively.
